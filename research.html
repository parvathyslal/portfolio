Research Profile

My research interests lie in Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing, with applications in areas such as cybersecurity and intelligent systems. I am particularly interested in building lightweight models for real-world scenarios.

Research Interests

My primary interests include Artificial Intelligence, Natural Language Processing (NLP), deep learning, and lightweight model design. I am especially interested in developing efficient and scalable models that can handle real-world data with limited computational resources.

Current Focus

Currently, I am working on lightweight and adaptive models, including a multimodal stress detection system using text, audio, and visual data. I have also explored deep learning approaches in cybersecurity, particularly in Network Intrusion Detection Systems.

Future Direction

In the future, I aim to work in NLP and advanced AI systems, while continuing to explore applications in domains such as cybersecurity, multimodal learning, and real-time decision systems.

Publications & Ongoing Work

Some of my past and ongoing works is as follows:

Deep Learning Based Network Intrusion Detection Systems

This work surveys deep learning approaches for Network Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS), highlighting their effectiveness in handling complex and evolving cyber threats. It reviews key models such as CNN, RNN, LSTM, and transformer-based methods, and discusses their advancements, limitations, and future research directions.

View Paper

Failure-Aware Adaptive Decision Algorithm for CNN–LSTM Based NIDS

This work proposes a failure-aware adaptive decision algorithm for CNN–LSTM based NIDS to address instability caused by fixed thresholds. By dynamically adjusting detection thresholds using short-term error patterns, the method reduces false positives while maintaining accuracy, improving reliability in real-world deployments.

Lightweight Multimodal Stress Detection System

This ongoing work focuses on developing a lightweight and adaptive multimodal system for stress detection using text, audio, and visual signals.
